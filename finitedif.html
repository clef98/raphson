Finite Difference Schemes classify several numerical techniques used for approximating derivatives with discretized
steps. Numerical techniques are a substitue for traditional analytical, or mathematical solutions of differential equations,
as their iterative nature leads to far more efficient computing speeds. Typically, the spatial and time domains of a system
are subject to discretization.

By definition, finite difference methods convert differential equations, often nonlinear in complex physical system,
to linear matrice equations convenient for computing and implementation. Nearly all finite difference schemes are derived
from Taylor's theorem, where its remainder term is assumed to be mathematically insignificant and we reach a derivative
generalization. As they are approximations, finite difference schemes are often developed in hand with measurements for
their round-off and truncation error.
