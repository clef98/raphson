The Newton-Raphson method is a fundamental technique generalized for findings roots of
non-linear systems. From an initial, often user-provided guess, a function is written in a standard
Taylor series expansion. The series is truncated at the second term due to the assumed proximity
of the guess and the accurate root(s). The derivatives are solved for and its extrapolation to the
x-axis grants a new approximation of the root. This process is iterated until convergence is reached
via a user-defined threshold. The Newton-Raphson method is quadratically convergent.
